Funding Landscape — Harris County & Fort Bend Corridor

Confidence is labeled per item; uncorroborated claims are flagged, not asserted. Content-farm sources were discarded in favor of official .gov documents and established local journalism.

How to read this

Two distinct fiscal worlds in one metro:

  • Harris County & Houston — large budgets, deficit-stressed. The money is in flood/infrastructure; the politics is in closing gaps.
  • Fort Bend trio (Missouri City, Stafford, Fort Bend County) — smaller, bond- and sales-tax-driven. One (Missouri City) has a live bond election forming now.

⏱️ Most time-sensitive item

Missouri City is standing up a November 2026 bond election. The window to shape what's in it runs now → July 20, 2026 (advisory committee), and Council must call the election by Aug 17 for the Nov 3 ballot. See the deep dive below.

Jurisdiction summaries

🏛️ Harris County — flood money is the story

After voters passed Proposition A (Nov 2024), the Harris County Flood Control District tax rate rose 58% ($0.031 → $0.0497 per $100), generating **$113M/year** for flood infrastructure — a historic maintenance-program expansion in the FY2026 budget. Taxable assessed value is compounding ~6.1%/year. Channel: flood mitigation is the open funding lane — tie asks to Prop A dollars. Confidence: High (local journalism + official budget). Sources: Houston Landing · Harris County FY2026 Adopted Budget Not verified: total FY2026 county budget figure (omitted rather than guessed).

🏙️ City of Houston — record deficit + mayor-vs-controller fight

Council passed a ~$7B all-funds budget, but City Controller Chris Hollins projects a $174M FY2025–26 deficit (topping 2025's record $145M); Mayor John Whitmire disputes it and pledges to balance without raising taxes. For FY2026–27, a projected ~$209M gap is being addressed (announced May 5, 2026) via structural moves: declaring Solid Waste a municipal utility, a $5 homeowner administration fee, and a right-of-way water fee (~$200M combined relief). A $65M federal public-safety grant tied to the 2026 FIFA World Cup offsets police/fire overtime; a voluntary retirement program saved ~$99M. Channel: every dollar is contested; the Whitmire–Hollins split is the live political fault line. Confidence: High on the dispute; the deficit number itself is deliberately contested — cite as a dispute, not a settled figure. Sources: Houston Public Media · Click2Houston · Community Impact

🟢 Fort Bend County — bond capacity, steady growth

Commissioners passed a ~$760M FY2026 budget (+3.9% YoY); the median homeowner pays $1,197 to the county + drainage district (+$56). Voters have authorized ~$1.4B in mobility/parks/flood-control bonds (incl. the 2023 program), now being drawn down; debt service ≈ 21% of the budget. Channel: substantial existing bond authority = projects already funded; track the mobility dashboard for what's queued. Confidence: High (local journalism + official budget). Sources: Houston Public Media · FBC Mobility Projects

🟡 Stafford — the no-property-tax tightrope

The largest Texas city with zero property tax since 1995; funded by sales tax (2% city share → 1.5% city / 0.5% SEDC; 8.25% combined rate). That structure leaves it exposed: a FY2025 ~$34.6M budget was balanced by drawing $1.7M from fund balance, and a measure to let residents vote on reinstating a property tax failed by a single vote last year. Council is weighing diverting Economic Development Corporation funds to cover services. Channel: structurally fragile to retail downturns; the property-tax question is politically radioactive and one vote from the ballot. Confidence: High (official + multiple outlets). Sources: Houston Public Media · ABC13 · City of Stafford — Tax Structure

🔎 Deep dive: Missouri City 2026 bond

Bottom line: A bond is being built now; the window to shape its contents closes before the vote. Influence happens now → July 20; after Aug 17 it's a campaign, not a selection.

Decision timeline

  • May 18, 2026 — Council directed staff to develop a 5-year capital-improvement bond program.
  • Now → mid-July — a Citizen Bond Advisory Committee (to be appointed) reviews/prioritizes projects.
  • July 20 — committee delivers final recommendations to Council.
  • By Aug 17 — Council must call the special election.
  • Nov 3, 2026 — election.
  • Amount: not yet set (committee-defined). Categories named by Public Works: mobility/street reconstruction, utilities, parks, facilities, public safety.
  • Official "2026 Bond Program: Proposed Projects" map (open directly; ArcGIS blocks automated extraction): https://storymaps.arcgis.com/stories/ea44e42715024e4f8c66c2edcf711f98
  • Source: Community Impact, May 19 2026

Delivery is slow — cuts both ways. As of 2023–24 reporting, Cave Run/Valley Manor were only reaching construction in Q4 2024 and the flagship Sta-Mo Sports Complex was still in design. Proponents tout the track record; opponents will hammer "still not built." Sources: 2021 results · 2023 progress

Likely 2026 centerpiece: drainage/flooding

The CIP spans drainage, transportation, traffic signals, bridges, parks, water/wastewater; the city has an active Watershed Master Plan Update and Storm Water Management Program. Given Fort Bend's flood history, a flood-resilience framing is the most defensible throughline. Sources: CIP · Watershed Master Plan Update

Fiscal capacity — verified vs. open

  • Charter max property-tax rate: $2.50/$100 (legal ceiling, not the adopted rate).
  • ⚠️ Not verified: the adopted FY2026 tax rate and total debt outstanding. Pull from Debt Obligations Transparency, Financial Transparency, and the ClearPoint Actual Tax Rate dashboard. New debt service + the still-amortizing $85.85M both bound how large a 2026 ask the rate can absorb.

Strategic read

  1. Be in the committee process before July 20 — that's where a project lives or dies.
  2. Lead with drainage/flood resilience + street reconstruction (proven 2021 winners and genuine need).
  3. Pre-empt the "slow delivery" attack with a delivery-accountability message.
  4. Anchor to Elackatt's stated priorities for mayoral buy-in.
