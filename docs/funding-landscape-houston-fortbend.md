> **Scope:** Harris County · City of Houston · Fort Bend County · Missouri City · Stafford
> **Researched:** late May / June 2026 · **Status:** point-in-time snapshot — *re-verify before relying on any figure*
> **Method:** multi-source web research with adversarial verification of load-bearing claims. Confidence is labeled per item; uncorroborated claims are flagged, not asserted. Content-farm sources were discarded in favor of official `.gov` documents and established local journalism.

---

## How to read this

Two distinct fiscal worlds in one metro:

- **Harris County & Houston** — large budgets, deficit-stressed. The money is in flood/infrastructure; the politics is in closing gaps.
- **Fort Bend trio (Missouri City, Stafford, Fort Bend County)** — smaller, bond- and sales-tax-driven. One (Missouri City) has a **live bond election forming now**.

---

## ⏱️ Most time-sensitive item

**Missouri City is standing up a November 2026 bond election.** The window to shape *what's in it* runs **now → July 20, 2026** (advisory committee), and Council must **call the election by Aug 17** for the **Nov 3** ballot. See the deep dive below.

## Jurisdiction summaries

### 🏛️ Harris County — flood money is the story
After voters passed **Proposition A (Nov 2024)**, the **Harris County Flood Control District** tax rate rose ~**58%** ($0.031 → $0.0497 per $100), generating **~$113M/year** for flood infrastructure — a historic maintenance-program expansion in the FY2026 budget. Taxable assessed value is compounding ~**6.1%/year**.
*Channel:* flood mitigation is the open funding lane — tie asks to Prop A dollars.
**Confidence:** High (local journalism + official budget).
Sources: [Houston Landing](https://houstonlanding.org/harris-county-flood-control-wants-a-tax-hike-to-fund-maintenance-work-voters-get-to-decide/) · [Harris County FY2026 Adopted Budget](https://budget.harriscountytx.gov/doc/Budget/budgetbook/FY2026/FY26_Adopted_Budget_Volume_I.pdf)
*Not verified:* total FY2026 county budget figure (omitted rather than guessed).

### 🏙️ City of Houston — record deficit + mayor-vs-controller fight
Council passed a **~$7B all-funds budget**, but **City Controller Chris Hollins** projects a **$174M FY2025–26 deficit** (topping 2025's record $145M); **Mayor John Whitmire disputes it** and pledges to balance **without raising taxes**. For **FY2026–27**, a projected **~$209M** gap is being addressed (announced May 5, 2026) via structural moves: declaring **Solid Waste a municipal utility**, a **$5 homeowner administration fee**, and a **right-of-way water fee** (~$200M combined relief). A **$65M federal public-safety grant** tied to the **2026 FIFA World Cup** offsets police/fire overtime; a voluntary retirement program saved ~$99M.
*Channel:* every dollar is contested; the Whitmire–Hollins split is the live political fault line.
**Confidence:** High on the dispute; the deficit *number itself* is deliberately contested — cite as a dispute, not a settled figure.
Sources: [Houston Public Media](https://www.houstonpublicmedia.org/articles/news/city-of-houston/2026/04/13/548963/city-of-houston-budget-deficit-mayor-john-whitmire/) · [Click2Houston](https://www.click2houston.com/news/local/2026/04/09/houston-controller-city-nowhere-close-to-balanced-budget-amid-projected-174m-shortfall-whitmire-disputes-figures/) · [Community Impact](https://communityimpact.com/houston/heights-river-oaks-montrose/government/2026/05/05/houston-to-implement-a-5-administration-fee-other-countermeasures-to-help-close-growing-budget-deficit/)

### 🟢 Fort Bend County — bond capacity, steady growth
Commissioners passed a **~$760M FY2026 budget** (+3.9% YoY); the median homeowner pays **$1,197** to the county + drainage district (+$56). Voters have authorized **~$1.4B** in mobility/parks/flood-control bonds (incl. the 2023 program), now being drawn down; **debt service ≈ 21%** of the budget.
*Channel:* substantial existing bond authority = projects already funded; track the mobility dashboard for what's queued.
**Confidence:** High (local journalism + official budget).
Sources: [Houston Public Media](https://www.houstonpublicmedia.org/articles/news/fort-bend/2025/09/24/531766/fort-bend-county-commissioners-pass-760-million-budget/) · [FBC Mobility Projects](https://www.fortbendcountytx.gov/government/departments/county-services/engineering/mobility-road-projects)

### 🟡 Stafford — the no-property-tax tightrope
The largest Texas city with **zero property tax since 1995**; funded by **sales tax** (2% city share → 1.5% city / 0.5% SEDC; 8.25% combined rate). That structure leaves it exposed: a **FY2025 ~$34.6M budget** was balanced by drawing **$1.7M from fund balance**, and a measure to let residents *vote* on reinstating a property tax **failed by a single vote** last year. Council is weighing **diverting Economic Development Corporation funds** to cover services.
*Channel:* structurally fragile to retail downturns; the property-tax question is politically radioactive and one vote from the ballot.
**Confidence:** High (official + multiple outlets).
Sources: [Houston Public Media](https://www.houstonpublicmedia.org/articles/news/fort-bend/2025/04/28/519874/stafford-could-divert-money-from-economic-development-to-cover-city-costs/) · [ABC13](https://abc13.com/post/stafford-texas-property-taxes-proposal-2-million-budget-shortfall/14982770/) · [City of Stafford — Tax Structure](https://www.staffordtx.gov/479/Tax-Structure)

## 🔎 Deep dive: Missouri City 2026 bond

**Bottom line:** A bond is being *built now*; the window to shape its contents closes before the vote. Influence happens **now → July 20**; after **Aug 17** it's a campaign, not a selection.

### Decision timeline
- **May 18, 2026** — Council directed staff to develop a **5-year capital-improvement bond program**.
- **Now → mid-July** — a **Citizen Bond Advisory Committee** (to be appointed) reviews/prioritizes projects.
- **July 20** — committee delivers **final recommendations** to Council.
- **By Aug 17** — Council must **call** the special election.
- **Nov 3, 2026** — election.
- **Amount:** *not yet set* (committee-defined). Categories named by Public Works: **mobility/street reconstruction, utilities, parks, facilities, public safety**.
- Official **"2026 Bond Program: Proposed Projects"** map (open directly; ArcGIS blocks automated extraction): https://storymaps.arcgis.com/stories/ea44e42715024e4f8c66c2edcf711f98
- Source: [Community Impact, May 19 2026](https://communityimpact.com/houston/sugar-land-missouri-city/government/2026/05/19/missouri-city-to-consider-calling-november-bond-election/)

### Who decides
Council = Mayor + 2 at-large + 4 district, elected every 3 years ([City Council](https://www.missouricitytx.gov/183/City-Council)):
- **Mayor:** Robin J. Elackatt — stated priorities: sidewalk repair, citywide infrastructure maintenance, public safety, business development.
- **At-Large #1:** Sonya Brown-Marshall · **At-Large #2 / Mayor Pro Tem:** Lynn Clouser
- **District A:** Monica Riley · **District B:** Sharita Thompson · **Districts C/D:** Joanna Ouderkirk and Floyd Emery
- ⚠️ *Verify the C-vs-D assignment* for Ouderkirk/Emery on the [Elected Officials page](https://www.missouricitytx.gov/414/Elected-Officials) — the source conflicted.
- The **Citizen Bond Advisory Committee** roster (once posted) is the key near-term influencer.

### The 2021 precedent ($85.85M, all three propositions passed)
| Prop | Purpose | Amount | Named projects |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Streets & mobility | $51.615M | 80+ streets; Lexington Blvd ~$7M; Cartwright Rd ~$5.5M; Valley Manor Dr $1.67M; Cave Run Dr $874K |
| B | Facilities | $11.22M | City building upgrades |
| C | Parks | $23.015M | Sta-Mo Sports Complex $10M; Freedom Tree Park $3.5M; trails at 5 parks |

**Delivery is slow — cuts both ways.** As of 2023–24 reporting, Cave Run/Valley Manor were only reaching construction in Q4 2024 and the flagship **Sta-Mo Sports Complex** was still in design. Proponents tout the track record; opponents will hammer "still not built."
Sources: [2021 results](https://communityimpact.com/houston/sugar-land-missouri-city/election/2021/11/02/unofficial-results-missouri-city-voters-pass-all-3-bond-propositions-in-8585-million-package/) · [2023 progress](https://communityimpact.com/houston/sugar-land-missouri-city/government/2023/02/14/missouri-city-makes-progress-on-2021-bond-projects/)

### Likely 2026 centerpiece: drainage/flooding
The CIP spans drainage, transportation, traffic signals, bridges, parks, water/wastewater; the city has an active **Watershed Master Plan Update** and Storm Water Management Program. Given Fort Bend's flood history, a **flood-resilience framing** is the most defensible throughline.
Sources: [CIP](https://www.missouricitytx.gov/87/Capital-Improvement-Program) · [Watershed Master Plan Update](https://www.missouricitytx.gov/1027/Watershed-Master-Plan-Update)

### Fiscal capacity — verified vs. open
- Charter **max** property-tax rate: **$2.50/$100** (legal ceiling, *not* the adopted rate).
- ⚠️ **Not verified:** the adopted FY2026 tax rate and total debt outstanding. Pull from [Debt Obligations Transparency](https://www.missouricitytx.gov/953/Debt-Obligations-Transparency), [Financial Transparency](https://www.missouricitytx.gov/529/Financial-Transparency), and the ClearPoint [Actual Tax Rate dashboard](https://missouricity.clearpointstrategy.com/responsible-city-government/actual-tax-rate/). New debt service + the still-amortizing $85.85M both bound how large a 2026 ask the rate can absorb.

### Strategic read
1. Be in the **committee process before July 20** — that's where a project lives or dies.
2. Lead with **drainage/flood resilience + street reconstruction** (proven 2021 winners and genuine need).
3. Pre-empt the **"slow delivery"** attack with a delivery-accountability message.
4. Anchor to **Elackatt's stated priorities** for mayoral buy-in.

## Confidence & caveats (whole document)
- **High confidence:** Stafford's tax structure; Houston's deficit dispute; Fort Bend's $760M budget; Missouri City's bond process & 2021 breakdown; Harris County's Prop A flood rate.
- **Developing / not fixed:** Missouri City 2026 bond **amount** (committee decides by July 20); Houston's deficit **figure** (deliberately contested — mayor vs. controller).
- **Not verified here (do not assert):** Harris County total FY2026 budget; Missouri City adopted FY2026 rate & debt; Missouri City District C/D assignment.
- **Federal note:** a separate finding from this engagement — **FEMA's BRIC program** was terminated (Apr 2025), ruled unlawfully terminated by a federal judge (Dec 11, 2025), and restored under a combined FY2024+FY2025 NOFO (~$1B) with narrower rules. Any flood-mitigation funding strategy that leans on BRIC must confirm the current NOFO status directly in FEMA GO.
